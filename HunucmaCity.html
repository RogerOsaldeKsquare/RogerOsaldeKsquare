Hunucmá City

General

Hunucmá is a population of Mexico in the state of Yucatán, head of the municipality of the same name, located in the northeast of the Yucatán peninsula. It has a population of 24,910 inhabitants. The main port of the municipality is Sisal located about 10 km. north of the municipal seat. It has 4 police stations: The aforementioned port of Sisal, San Antonio Chel, Huncanab, Texán!

History

The exact data on the founding of the city of Hunucmá are unknown, it is said that it is the sacred land of Hunab Ku that was in dispute for years between Mayan groups that sought to dominate the strategic position of the salt flow towards the interior of the peninsula. It is known that before the conquest of Yucatan, the region where the city is located belonged to the province of Ah Canul. After the conquest, the area was under the regime of the encomiendas, including that of Beatriz de Montejo in 1571 who was later inherited by her husband, who was governor of Yucatán from 1571 to 1573, Diego de Santillán and Pineda.

The Most Famous food of Hunucmá

Sopes de Queso de bola

Taste and tradition in the municipality of Hunucmá with the famous sopes ‘Hunucmá style’, are the creation of decades of lonchería “El maya” small business of the municipality that has enhanced the name of the snack bar. The specialty of the house is the sopes of breaded, black filling and chicken, accompanied with Queso de bola, attracting the palate of the hundreds of diners who come to the place to taste these sopes that have become a gastronomic tradition.
